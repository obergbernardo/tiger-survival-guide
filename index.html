How to Survive in a Boat with a Bengal Tiger

Method 1: Avoid Getting Into This Boat Trip in the First Place

  • Step 1: Don’t go on boat trips with wild animals. Tigers don’t belong on boats. Resist the temptation. Don’t click sketchy links. If someone invites you, say you have a tennis tournament. It always works.
  • Step 2: If you're already in a boat with a tiger and it’s sinking—pause. Think. Land away from the tiger, ideally near food and shelter. Snacks = survival.
Situation One: You Landed Right Next to the Tiger

Congrats! You messed up your physics and wish you’d paid attention in Grade 6 trigonometry instead of playing Brawl Stars. You’re face-to-face with a hungry tiger. This guide can't help you anymore. Accept your fate peacefully.

Situation Two: You Landed Away from the Tiger but Far from Food and Shelter
  • Step 1: Build a raft using buoys and ropes. Don’t know how? Too bad. Every human should know this.
  • Step 2: Push the raft at least 150 meters away. Tigers jump far.
  • Step 3: Pull out your phone, turn on TELUS mobile data, close Brawl Stars, and call 911. Yes, they handle tiger-boat situations now.
  • Step 4: Wait. And try not to think about the tiger. Good luck.
Situation Three: You Landed Near the Food and Shelter, but the Tiger Didn’t
  • Step 1: Toss the food off the boat. Tigers are not geniuses—they’ll go after it.
  • Step 2: Swim around and push the boat toward safety with your legs.
  • Step 3: Realize you’re freezing in the Pacific. Hypothermia kicks in. If you’re gym-strong, maybe you climb back in. But you’ll probably still freeze. At least you read this guide!

Final Thoughts

  • If someone invites you to a boat trip, say no. Immediately.
  • Don’t try to pet the tiger. It’s not your cat, Cookie.
  • Use your massive brain (and some physics) to land in the right spot.
  • Survival odds are low, but now you’re semi-prepared.
Good luck! You’re gonna need it.
